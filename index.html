about me

I am a second-year PhD student at the School of Applied Mathematics from the Getulio Vargas Foundation (FGV - EMAp by its acronym in Portuguese) advised by Prof. Jorge Poco. My research is focused on geometric deep learning and explainable AI and their applications to spatial-temporal forecasting and neuroscience.

I did my Bachelor at the Catholic University San Pablo where I wrote my thesis under the supervision of Prof. Regina Ticona and Prof. Yudith Cardinale. In my last year I also participated in a research project as a Researcher Student, working together with Prof. Yudith Cardinale, Prof. Irvin Dongo and Prof. Jose Dias Amado, we perform a research in recognizing human emotions from images using deep learning and applied it in robots. Also, previously I prarticipated at the computer vision group of Prof. Renso Mora, where I research deep learning for human pose estimation from images.

I am from Arequipa - Peru, now in Rio de Janeiro - Brazil. I like research, and look for understanding a bit better how graph neural networks solve problems, and how the brain works.
